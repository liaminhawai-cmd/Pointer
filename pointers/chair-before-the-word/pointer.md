# The Chair Before the Word

You look at a chair and you instantly know it's a chair.

But do you know it *before* you think the word "chair"?

If you don't — then how can you recognise a chair while you're in the middle of talking about a table? The word "chair" isn't in your mind, but the chair is still obviously a chair.

And if you do recognise it without the word — then what is it that recognises the chair before the label arrives? What is that wordless knowing?

Try it on whatever is in front of you right now. A cup, your hand, the screen. See if you can catch the tiny gap where the shape is already known, before the label drops onto it like a stamp.
