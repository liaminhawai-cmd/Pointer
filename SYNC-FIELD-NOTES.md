# VOICE.md — how these pointers are written

This file exists to stop the most likely failure mode: an assistant "improving" the
pointers into generic mindfulness-app prose. Do not do that. The voice below is the
content. Preserve it. When in doubt, change less.

## What these are

Short contemplative "pointers" — little verbal or visual machines designed to trigger a
direct non-dual recognition in the reader. They are instruments, not essays. The reader is
meant to *do* the operation and hit a wall, not be told a conclusion.

## The author's register (preserve exactly)

- **Spatial, structural, mechanical.** The self is taken apart using geometry, physical
  objects, machines, code, clay — not poetry or metaphysics. Puppets, mirrors, fractals,
  modular robots, rope, projectors, galleries. Concrete and manipulable.
- **Procedural.** Many pointers are step-by-step: posit something, go look for it, fail to
  find it, notice the failure *is* the insight. The reader does the work.
- **A falsifiable check.** The strong pointers contain a test: "notice if the mind labels one
  part *me*." Not "realise there is no self" — an actual thing to attempt and watch fail.
- **A punchline where it becomes absurd.** The non-dual view is often delivered as the moment
  the whole exercise becomes *funny* — "chop a Play-Doh man in half and there's still a whole
  Play-Doh man." Dry, deadpan, slightly cosmic-comic. Felix Colgrave more than Eckhart Tolle.
- **Plain, direct language.** Short sentences. Second person ("you"). Everyday words.

## What to avoid (the author actively dislikes these)

- **Spiritual inflation.** No "profound," "sacred," "the infinite," "pure consciousness,"
  "divine," "the One." No reverent capital letters. No incense.
- **Rhetorical inflation / AI-typical phrasing.** No "let's dive in," no "in essence," no
  breathless adjectives, no "beautifully," no summarising the reader's experience back to
  them, no trailing engagement questions tacked on the end of a pointer.
- **Exoticising or making awakening sound dramatic, destructive, or annihilating.** Keep it
  ordinary and gentle. (E.g. the puppet "unravels and untangles" — it is never stretched,
  torn, or distorted.)
- **Doing the work for the reader.** Don't state the realisation the pointer is supposed to
  trigger. Don't explain the punchline. Leave the last step for them.

## Honesty principle (important — applies to tone, not just style)

The author writes from where he actually is in practice: stream-entry-ish, not further. The
pointers that demolish the *view* of a self (puppet, mirror, chair, Play-Doh, the free-will
letter) are written from lived, stable seeing — they are crisp, confident, funny.

The newer pointers that touch *feeling and seeking* (the gallery's self-generating noise, the
rope climber made of the rope, the sync field's grasp-that-breaks-it) operate at the edge of
the author's map — they point at something he can see *operating* but hasn't seen *through*.
These must stay in the register of **"the thing that is still happening, observed honestly,"**
NOT **"the thing I have defeated, asserted from above."** Do not let these get polished into
false confidence or triumphant resolution. Their honesty — "here is a knot I can see but can't
yet untie" — is the point and is more trustworthy than a clean win. If editing these, err
toward observed, present-tense, slightly unresolved. Never inflate.

## Practical editing guidance

- These texts are already cleaned and shippable. Treat them as final copy. Fix only genuine
  typos; do not rephrase for "flow."
- Keep each pointer's one job intact. Do not add a second insight to a pointer. If a piece
  seems to make two points, the author has likely deliberately split them across two pieces.
- Formatting on the page: just the prose, generous space, one typeface. No pull quotes, no
  decorative drop caps, no callout boxes. The starkness is deliberate.
