# The Letter

Imagine you're reading a letter, and you're about to write back. It feels like you have the free will to choose what you do. Let's look at what actually happens.

As you type, your fingers move and click the keys — do *they* have free will? The muscles contracting to move your fingers — do they?

There are electrical signals. There are muscles moving. There are thoughts appearing in your mind before you type. But did you think those thoughts before you thought them? Did you choose a thought before it arose? And if you did — did you choose to choose it, before you chose it?

Now look at the other end. There's light hitting your eyes. There are signals running from your eyes to your brain. Do you choose to read? Can you choose *not* to read, when you look straight at text?

If there's no chooser anywhere in this — if it's all just happening on its own — then it's no different from dropping a ball and watching it bounce. It's just things happening. Which is exactly how the universe works: a sequence of things happening.

There doesn't need to be a separate doer. There are just causes, and effects.
